Here's exactly what to expect.

Four straightforward steps. No surprises. You stay in control from first contact to closing.

The four steps

1

Share a few details.

Use the short form on the next page to tell us about the property and your situation. We ask for a property address, property type, ownership status, and a loose sense of your timeline. The form takes about two minutes and works on mobile.

You don't need an appraisal, a survey, or any documents to get started.

2

We review confidentially — no automated number.

A real person on our team reviews the property, the comparable sales in your market, and the details you shared. This usually takes a few hours to one business day.

We don't use an algorithm that spits out a lowball estimate. We look at each property honestly, because the goal is a conversation — not a trap.

3

We have a private conversation.

We schedule a call (or text, if you prefer) at a time that works for you. On that call, we answer any questions you have, walk through the options we see, and explain what a sale would look like — including a realistic number, timeline, and what you'd net after closing costs.

If something doesn't make sense for us to buy, we tell you that directly and, where we can, point you toward other resources.

4

You decide what's next — with no deadline.

If a sale makes sense and our offer works for you, we prepare a straightforward purchase agreement and move at your pace. Some owners close in two to three weeks. Others take months — because of probate timelines, family coordination, or simply because they weren't in a rush. Both are fine.

If you decide not to sell, we leave you with information you can use regardless of what you do next. No hard feelings, no follow-up pressure.

What we don't do.

We think it's worth being specific about what this process isn't.

We don't list on the MLS.

This is not a traditional listing. Your property will not appear on Zillow, Realtor.com, or any public site. We're a direct buyer — not a brokerage.

We don't charge commissions.

There is no listing agreement and no commission. What you agree to is what you receive, less only standard closing costs (title, escrow, etc.).

We don't require repairs or cleanouts.

We are comfortable buying properties as-is, including properties that need significant work, still contain belongings, or have deferred maintenance.

We don't pressure you to decide quickly.

A private conversation is information — not a commitment. Many owners come to us months before they're ready, and that's a perfectly normal way to start.

Common questions about the process.

How do you determine what to offer?

We look at comparable sales in your area, the property's condition, any liens or tax balances we'd be absorbing, carrying costs, and our cost to renovate or reposition. We share our thinking transparently on the call.

Is there a contract I have to sign before the call?

No. The initial conversation is completely informal. If and when we both agree on terms, we put together a purchase agreement — which you should always have reviewed by your own attorney before signing.

Can I be in probate or co-own with someone who isn't onboard yet?

Yes. We work through probate timelines routinely. We're patient, and we can talk through the process with one heir while others are still deciding. We don't require all owners to be ready at once to have the first conversation.

Do you pay closing costs?

Typically yes — we cover standard closing costs on our side and often absorb the seller's, too. The exact terms depend on the transaction. We explain everything before you sign anything.

What if your offer is lower than what the market bears?

A direct-buyer offer typically reflects the cost of as-is purchase, no commissions, and a faster close — which is a meaningful trade-off for some owners, and not for others. If a traditional listing would serve you better, we'll say so.
